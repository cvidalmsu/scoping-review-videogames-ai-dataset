# AI-Assisted Tools in the Video Game Development Process (2020–2025)

This repository contains the datasets, coding files, bibliographic metadata, and supplementary materials associated with the scoping review:

> **AI-Assisted Tools in the Video Game Development Process: A Scoping Review (2020–2025)**

---

# 📊 Overview

This study systematically analyzes research published between **2020 and 2025**, identifying how artificial intelligence (AI) techniques are applied to support different stages of the video game development lifecycle.

The review follows the **PRISMA-ScR framework** and includes a final corpus of **135 primary studies** after a multi-stage screening and eligibility process.

The repository provides the material required to support transparency, reproducibility, and future extensions of the review.

# 🧪 Review Scope

The review maps AI-assisted approaches applied to video game development, including:

- Procedural Content Generation (PCG)
- Level and environment generation
- Narrative generation and dialogue systems
- AI-assisted design workflows
- Non-Player Character (NPC) behavior modeling
- Automated playtesting and quality assurance
- Human–AI co-creative systems
- Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs)

---

# 📜 Inclusion Criteria

Studies were included if they:

- Focused on AI-assisted tools or systems applied to video game development
- Presented technical, methodological, or software-oriented contributions
- Were published between 2020 and 2025
- Included validation, experimentation, or practical application

---

# ❌ Exclusion Criteria

Studies were excluded if they:

- Focused exclusively on education, healthcare, or simulation contexts unrelated to game development
- Lacked technical or methodological detail
- Did not include implementation, evaluation, or validation
- Were inaccessible or incomplete

---

# 🔍 Methodology

The review follows the PRISMA-ScR methodology, including:

1. Identification of studies across multiple databases
2. Duplicate removal
3. Title and abstract screening
4. Full-text eligibility assessment
5. Structured data extraction and coding

The final dataset includes:

- **697 identified records**
- **293 duplicate records removed**
- **404 screened records**
- **148 reports sought for retrieval**
- **13 reports not retrieved**
- **135 included studies**

---

# 📈 Data Coding Dimensions

Each included study was classified according to five analytical dimensions:

- **Technical Aspect (TA)**
- **Methodological Design (MD)**
- **Application Domain (AD)**
- **Validation Approach (VA)**
- **Reported Contributions (RC)**

Detailed coding schemes are available in the supplementary material.
